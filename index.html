Summary from the book: "The Lorax" by Dr. Seuss

  • The story begins with a boy visiting a barren place and meeting the Once-ler.
  • The Once-ler discovers the Truffula Trees, Bar-ba-loots, and clean air.
  • The Lorax appears and warns the Once-ler to stop cutting the trees.
  • The Once-ler ignores the Lorax and continues cutting the trees for profit.
  • The environment is destroyed as the animals and clean air disappear.
  • The last Truffula Tree is cut down, and the Once-ler's factory shuts down.
  • The Lorax leaves, leaving behind a message: 'UNLESS.'
  • The boy is given the last Truffula seed to restore the forest.
  • The message emphasizes that individual action can make a difference.
  • The book ends on a hopeful note that nature can be saved.
